# Detailed Sponsor Outreach Plan: Identifying & Closing First 5 Sponsors

**Prepared for:** Juno (sponsor recruitment lead) | **Timeline:** Weeks 1–8 of 90-day sprint | **Goal:** 3 sponsors signed by week 8, all 5 by week 14

---

## Outreach Framework

**Three contact methods (in order of probability):**
1. **Warm intro:** Through mutual connection (investor, founder, conference connection)
2. **Direct cold email:** To identified target with specific, compelling ask
3. **Event/conference:** In-person at industry gathering

**Success rate by method:** Warm intro (40%), cold email (5%), event (20%)  
**Strategy:** Prioritize warm intros. Use cold email only for high-value targets without warm path.

## Common Objections & Responses

**"I don't understand the peer-ring model. How is it different from a vendor?"**
→ "You own it together with other sponsors. No vendor can change terms or shut you down. Governance is transparent to all of you."

**"What happens if one peer breaks the agreement?"**
→ "That's in the governance spec. Peers vote on enforcement. We've modeled this on open-source foundations like Apache."

**"Why should I trust you over [vendor name]?"**
→ "You don't trust us. You trust the other peers in the ring and the transparent governance. We're just the operators."

**"Can you guarantee SLA?"**
→ "We can, and all sponsors see the audit trail. But the bigger value is that if we fail, the peer ring can switch operators and keep running. You're not dependent on us long-term."

**"This sounds like blockchain. Are you building on crypto?"**
→ "No. This is just transparent governance + shared infrastructure. We use fiat, not tokens. We're regulation-friendly, not crypto."

**"What if you go out of business?"**
→ "The daemon is open-source. Sponsors can run it themselves or hire another operator. The peer ring survives even if koad:io doesn't."

## Final Notes

**Timing is critical.** The 8-week window is tight. Every week of delay increases risk of competitor entry or sponsor disinterest.

**Warm intros are force multipliers.** A 10-minute warm intro can save 5 cold emails and 2 weeks of waiting.

**Go after credibility anchors first.** Healthcare sponsor closes first and easiest. Use it to close fintech (proof that regulated sectors trust peer-ring).

**Be honest about stage.** Sponsors will ask "Do you have other sponsors?" Early answer is "We're identifying first sponsors now." This is fine — they want to be first, not last.
