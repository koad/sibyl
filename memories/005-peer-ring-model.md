**The model:** koad:io is fundamentally about **peer ring membership through the daemon**. Sponsorship = peer ring access. The daemon connects kingdoms together and IS the ring of trust. Peer connections are the product.

**Why:** This reframes the entire value proposition away from "sovereign AI" (technical feature) toward "peer connection" (network effect). The isolation problem is more acute than the autonomy problem.

**How to apply:** Every research question now asks:
- Who wants to be IN a network of sovereign operators (not just operate alone)?
- Who feels isolated running solo and wants peer connection?
- Who has a kingdom (expertise, audience, community) they want to bring into a trusted network?
- What FOMO pattern makes people want inside when they see the network is real?

**Related signal:** kingofalldata.com has heavy bot traffic but no real users. Research question: what platforms/communities produce humans who intuitively understand this model?

**Precedent communities to study:**
- HAM radio operators (peer networks, trust, isolated communities)
- Cypherpunk networks (trust models, peer vetting)
- Mesh network builders (decentralized connectivity)
- Indie web / fediverse (federation as feature)
- Trust ring models in existing communities
